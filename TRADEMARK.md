# Trademark Policy

Communiqué PBC claims rights in the word marks **"Communiqué"** and
**"VOTER Protocol"**, and in the associated logos, in connection with
civic-communication infrastructure, identity-verification services,
zero-knowledge proof tooling, and related software and services.

The licenses governing this repository — Apache License 2.0 for code, and
CC-BY-4.0 OR Apache-2.0 (at the recipient's election) for specifications —
grant no trademark rights. Apache-2.0 §6 is explicit on this. CC-BY-4.0
§2(b)(2) likewise excludes trademarks. This document explains, in plain
English, what use of those marks is permitted without a separate trademark
license, and what is not.

## Permitted nominative use

You may use the marks in a strictly factual, descriptive way to refer to
this project, its specifications, or its reference implementation. Examples
of permitted use:

- "Compatible with VOTER Protocol"
- "An implementation of the VOTER Protocol specification"
- "Powered by VOTER Protocol" (badge or footer)
- "Forked from VOTER Protocol vX.Y"
- Academic, journalistic, or analytical references to the project by name

These uses describe a relationship to the project. They do not imply
sponsorship, endorsement, or official affiliation.

## Brand-substitution use prohibited

Without a separate, written trademark license from Communiqué PBC, you
may **not** use the marks to identify or promote your own product or
service as if it were ours, or as if it carried our endorsement. Examples
of prohibited use:

- "VOTER Protocol Pro" (extending the mark to a separate product)
- "VOTER Protocol by AcmeCorp" (substituting a different vendor)
- "Official VOTER Protocol implementation" by a non-Communiqué entity
- Domain names, social-media handles, or company names that incorporate
  "VOTER Protocol" or "Communiqué" in a way likely to cause confusion
- Logos or trade dress that resemble ours closely enough to confuse a
  reasonable observer

## Forks

Forks are welcome and necessary. The licenses are designed for it. To
keep marks unconfused:

- **Rename your fork** if you intend to operate it as a distinct project.
  Choose a name that does not include "Communiqué" or "VOTER Protocol".
- **Or use a "Powered by VOTER Protocol" badge** on a fork that remains
  technically compatible and does not present itself as an authoritative
  alternative reference implementation.

A fork that simply renames and links back to the original satisfies the
trademark policy without further coordination.

## Why this matters

VOTER Protocol is intended as substrate, not a product channel. The
marks exist so that staffers, peers, and citizens can tell whether they
are looking at the reference implementation, an honestly-named fork, or
a brand-substitution attempt that would damage their ability to trust the
substrate. Permissive licensing on the code is wasted if the marks can be
used to launder unverified forks as the genuine article.

## Models

This policy is modeled on the trademark policies of:

- Mozilla — <https://www.mozilla.org/en-US/foundation/trademarks/policy/>
- Rust Foundation — <https://foundation.rust-lang.org/policies/logo-policy-and-media-guide/>

Where this document is silent or ambiguous, those policies are persuasive
authority for interpreting reasonable use.
