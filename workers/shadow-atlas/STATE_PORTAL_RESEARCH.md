## Key Findings

### Redistricting Timeline
- **Most states:** 2021-2022 redistricting cycle following 2020 Census
- **Court-ordered revisions:** OH (2023), WI (2024), WA (2024), ND (2024)
- **Latest redistricting:** AK (2023-05-15), WA District 15 (2024-03-15)

### Data Sources
- **State official sources:** 23 states have direct state GIS portals
- **Census TIGER/Line:** Reliable fallback for all 50 states
- **Hybrid approach:** Many states use both (state portal for freshness, Census for standardization)

### Special Cases
- **Nebraska:** Unicameral legislature (49 districts, no separate House)
- **New Hampshire:** Floterial districts require TWO House files (base + overlay)
- **Idaho, New Jersey, North Dakota:** Same district boundaries for both chambers
- **Arizona:** IRC uses same boundaries for both chambers

### Freshness Priority (< 36 months since redistricting)
States redistricted within last 36 months (as of 2025-11-10):
- Alaska (2023-05-15): 30 months
- Ohio (2023-09-29): 25 months
- Wisconsin (2024-02-19): 21 months
- Washington District 15 (2024-03-15): 20 months

All other states are 36+ months post-redistricting, so TIGER/Line is acceptable.
